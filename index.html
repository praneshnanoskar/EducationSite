Education in India is a Concurrent List subject, that is both the Indian central government, and the state governments have responsibility for enacting and implementing education policy.[35] The central board and most of the state boards uniformly follow the "10+2" pattern of education.[36]: 3  In this pattern, study of 10 years is done in schools and 2 years in Junior colleges (Mumbai, Maharashtra),[36]: 44  and then 3 years of study for a bachelor's degree for college.[37] The first 10 years is further subdivided into 4 years of primary education, 6 years of High School followed by 2 years of Junior colleges.[36]: 5  This pattern originated from the recommendation of the Education Commission of 1964–66.[38]
